This document explains what is — and what is **not** — covered by the
AGPL-3.0 license that ships with this repository. It is written in
plain English. It is not a legal contract, but it is the formal,
public position of the VeilChat project on use of its name and brand.

## TL;DR

- The **source code** in this repository is licensed under
  [AGPL-3.0-only](./LICENSE). You may use, modify, and redistribute it
  on the terms of that license.
- The **VeilChat brand** — the names "VeilChat", "Veil Protocol", the
  VeilChat logo (see [`brand/veilchat-logo.png`](./brand/veilchat-logo.png)),
  and the visual identity associated with them — is **not** licensed
  under AGPL-3.0. All rights are reserved.
- If you fork this repository or build a product on top of this code,
  **you must rename your fork or product** and use your own branding.
  You may not call it "VeilChat", "Veil", or any name confusingly
  similar, and you may not use the VeilChat logo.

This is the same model used by Signal, Mozilla / Firefox, PostgreSQL,
WordPress, and most major open-source projects with a brand to protect.
The code is open. The brand is not.

## What you may do

- **Fork the code, modify it, and redistribute it** under AGPL-3.0,
  as long as you comply with the license.
- **Refer to VeilChat by name** in factual, descriptive ways: in
  documentation, blog posts, security write-ups, news articles,
  academic papers, audit reports, or comparisons. For example,
  "tested for compatibility with VeilChat" or "based on the VeilChat
  Protocol audit" is fine.
- **State, accurately, that your fork is derived from the VeilChat
  protocol code** — for example, "Forked from veil-protocol" or
  "Based on the VeilChat Protocol implementation."

## What you may not do

- **You may not name your fork, product, app, service, company, or
  domain "VeilChat", "Veil", "Veil Protocol", or any name confusingly
  similar** (including translations, transliterations, and common
  misspellings — e.g. "VeilApp", "Veil Messenger", "VeilSecure",
  "Veal Chat", "Vail Chat", etc.).
- **You may not use the VeilChat logo** in your fork, product,
  marketing, app icon, store listing, or any other context that

- **You may not use the VeilChat name or logo as the primary brand

- **You may not imply endorsement, affiliation, sponsorship, or

- **You may not register domain names, social media handles, or

  confusingly similar variants for a messaging product.

## Why these rules exist

VeilChat publishes the cryptographic core of its messenger so that
anyone can independently verify the security claims of the product.
That transparency is only meaningful if users can trust that the app
they download from "VeilChat" on the App Store is actually the audited
client built by the VeilChat team — not a reskinned fork with a
backdoor. Reserving the brand is what makes the audit story credible.

The code is published so it can be inspected and reused. The brand is
reserved so users can tell who they are actually talking to.

## Reporting brand abuse

If you see a project, fork, app, website, or social media account
misusing the VeilChat name or logo — for example, a copycat app on
the Play Store using our logo, or a phishing site using our name —
please report it to **brand@veil.app** with as much detail as you
can: links, screenshots, app store IDs, and any context.

We will investigate and, where appropriate, pursue takedowns through
the relevant platform (app store report, registrar abuse, hosting
provider, DMCA / trademark notice) and, if necessary, legal action.
